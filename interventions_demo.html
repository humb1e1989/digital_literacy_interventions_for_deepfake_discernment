🕵️ Deepfake Discernment Study

CHI 2026 · Geissler, Robertson & Feuerriegel

Designing Effective Digital Literacy Interventions for Deepfake Discernment

N = 1,200 U.S. participants, randomly assigned to one of six conditions. After their assigned training (or none), all participants completed a 15-image detection task. Two weeks later they repeated the task with a new image set.

Key result: The Textual (+7.5 pp) and Visual (+13 pp) interventions significantly improved deepfake detection accuracy. Gamified, Feedback, and Knowledge showed no significant improvement at T1. No intervention significantly reduced trust in real images.

Condition: Control

No Intervention

Participants in the control condition receive no training. They proceed immediately to the image discernment task. In the actual study, they saw the same introduction text as all other participants (explaining that real = shot by a human; fake = AI-generated), then classified 15 images.

Intervention 1 · Knowledge

AI-Image Generation

Some of the images you see online could be generated by artificial intelligence (AI) and are hence fake. Here, we want to teach you how AI generates fake images. Later, we will ask you to apply your knowledge in a fake image detection task.

Please read the following paragraph carefully:

AI can learn how to generate realistic images by looking at millions of example pictures. After inputting the example images into the AI, they are transformed into representations that only computers can understand and that don't mean anything to humans. From those, AI then learns patterns of how the world, such as objects, people, and places look like. After training, humans can ask AI models to generate images through simple input text, this is called prompting. The possibilities for AI-generated images are endless, for example, AI can generate things that have never been seen before such as made-up scenarios or even attempt to imitate real people or places.

This is the complete text shown to participants (Table 4 in the paper). No images are shown. The paper found no significant improvement in deepfake detection accuracy (padj = 0.980).

Intervention 2 · Textual

How to Detect Fake Images

Some of the images you see online could be generated by artificial intelligence (AI) and are hence fake. Here, we show you 5 ways to detect fake images that were generated by AI. Please read the following hints very carefully.

In the actual study, each hint appeared on its own separate page in randomised order. No images are shown in this condition. Result: +7.5 pp (padj < 0.01).

Intervention 3 · Visual

How to Detect Fake Images

Some of the images you see online could be generated by artificial intelligence (AI) and are hence fake. Here, we show you 5 ways to detect fake images that were generated by AI. Each hint is accompanied by an example image. Please read the following hints very carefully.

Each hint appeared on its own page in randomised order. Example images were generated with DALL-E 3 (except tip 5: the viral Pope Francis deepfake). Result: +13 pp (padj < 0.001).

Intervention 4 · Gamified

🎮 Let's Play a Game!

Some of the images you see online could be generated by AI and are hence fake. Here, we show you 5 ways to detect fake images. Each hint is accompanied by an example image.

How the game works:

  • 📕 Read the hint.
  • 🖱️ Click on the area of the image where you locate the mentioned error.
  • ✅ If you find the error, you receive 10 points and a green circle marks the correct area.
  • ❌ If you click the wrong area, you see "Not quite! Try again." in red — keep clicking until you find it or the timer runs out.
  • ⏳ If the timer runs out, you receive no points for that round.
  • 🏆 You can collect up to 50 points in total. You have 30 seconds per image.

Result: no significant improvement (padj = 0.310). Possible explanation: gamification may have diverted attention from learning the cues.

Intervention 5 · Feedback

Training: How to Detect Fake Images

Welcome to our fake image detection training module! In this training session:

  • We'll show you 10 images (5 real + 5 fake).
  • For each image, decide if it's real or fake.
  • You'll receive immediate feedback after each answer.

After completing this training, you'll apply your new skills in the actual detection task.

Unlike the other interventions, no descriptions of errors are provided — only exposure and feedback. This tests whether repeated exposure alone supports learning. Result: no significant improvement (padj = 1.000).
